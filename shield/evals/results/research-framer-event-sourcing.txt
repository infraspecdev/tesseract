## Research Framing

### Stakeholders

- **Payments platform engineers (backend, India + EU teams)** maintaining transaction ledger services — care about implementation complexity, replay semantics, schema evolution, and on-call burden when projections drift.
- **Principal/staff engineers and architects** owning the multi-year persistence strategy — care about reversibility of the architectural commitment, vendor lock-in (EventStoreDB vs. Kafka-as-log vs. roll-your-own on Postgres), and how ES interacts with existing CDC/outbox patterns.
- **Compliance and regulatory affairs leads (India RBI, EU MiFID II, global GDPR)** — care about whether the architecture provably satisfies the RBI 24-hour transaction reporting / NPCI reconciliation rules, MiFID II Article 25 transaction-reconstruction obligations, and GDPR Article 17 right-to-erasure on an append-only store.
- **Risk and internal audit** — care about non-repudiation, tamper-evidence of the ledger, and whether replay can reproduce a state-as-of any past timestamp for dispute investigation.
- **SREs / platform reliability** — care about recovery semantics (replay time vs. snapshot cadence), storage growth, and operational complexity of running an event store in production.
- **Data engineering / analytics** — care about downstream projection consumers, schema versioning of events, and the cost of building read models for finance/regulatory dashboards.
- **Product engineering managers** committing roadmap — care about delivery velocity impact and ramp time for new hires onto a CQRS+ES codebase.
- **CTO / VP Engineering (sponsor)** — owns the build-vs-buy and adopt-vs-defer decision; needs a defensible position to present to the board and to regulators.

### Decision(s) to Make

1. **Adopt / partially adopt / defer**: Should the financial transaction core be re-platformed onto an event-sourced architecture, or should we instead adopt a narrower pattern (transactional outbox, append-only audit log alongside a CRUD system of record, or change data capture into an event log)?
2. **If adopting, what is the scope boundary**: Which bounded contexts (e.g., ledger postings, payment authorization, reconciliation, dispute) are event-sourced, and which remain state-stored?
3. **Storage substrate**: EventStoreDB, Kafka-as-event-store, Axon Server, or a Postgres-based hand-rolled event store — which trade-off profile fits our reliability, regulatory, and team-skill envelope?
4. **GDPR-vs-append-only resolution strategy**: Crypto-shredding, tokenization with external PII vault, or event-level redaction with a documented compensating control — which is defensible to a Data Protection Authority?
5. **Replay & recovery SLOs**: What snapshot cadence and replay-time budget are we committing to, and what does that imply for storage and compute sizing?

### Success Criteria

Research is considered successful when the synthesis enables the sponsor to:

1. State, in one paragraph each, the **adopt / partial-adopt / defer** recommendation with named trade-offs, defensible to a technical board.
2. Produce a **regulatory-fit table** mapping each obligation (RBI 24h reconciliation, MiFID II Article 25 reconstruction, GDPR Article 17 erasure, PCI-DSS logging) to a concrete architectural mechanism (snapshot policy, projection rebuild, crypto-shredding, tokenization, etc.) with at least one primary-source citation per row.
3. Name **at least three failure modes** observed by other teams (with citations) and our proposed mitigation for each.
4. Quantify, even roughly, **replay time and storage growth** at 1x, 5x, and 25x current daily transaction volume, with sources for the assumed throughput numbers.
5. Identify **at least one credible dissenting voice** (Udi Dahan style "don't do this unless…") and engage their argument rather than dismiss it.
6. Surface **direct quotes from Young, Fowler, and Vernon** integrated into the body — not only in references.

### Prioritized Research Questions

1. **(Highest impact — regulatory-fit)** How does an append-only event store reconcile with GDPR Article 17 right-to-erasure, and what is the current ICO/CNIL/DPA enforcement posture on crypto-shredding and tokenization as compensating controls? — *This is the question most likely to kill or reshape the architecture; if the answer is "DPAs reject crypto-shredding for PII," adoption scope shrinks dramatically.*
2. **(Regulatory-fit, India-specific)** Does event sourcing demonstrably satisfy RBI's 24-hour reporting/reconciliation rules and NPCI UPI reconciliation requirements better, worse, or equivalently to a CRUD + outbox pattern? — *Drives the partial-adopt scope: ledger-postings yes, customer-data no.*
3. **(Reconstruction obligation)** What does MiFID II Article 25 + ESMA RTS 22 demand for transaction reconstruction, and is event-sourced replay a superior evidentiary substrate compared to traditional audit logs? — *Determines whether ES is a compliance asset or a compliance-neutral cost.*
4. **(Two-way-door check)** What patterns and concrete failure modes do teams that adopted and later regretted event sourcing in fintech report, and what early signals predicted those regrets? — *Decides whether this is a reversible bet.*
5. **(Storage-substrate)** Comparing EventStoreDB, Kafka-as-event-store, Axon Server, and Postgres-hand-rolled on (a) ordering guarantees, (b) replay throughput, (c) operational maturity in regulated environments, (d) team-skill availability in India and EU markets — which substrate fits? — *Directly informs build-vs-buy.*
6. **(Replay & recovery SLOs)** What snapshot cadences and projection-rebuild times do production fintech systems report at our scale tier, and what does that imply for our recovery SLOs? — *Operationalizes the adoption.*
7. **(Event schema evolution)** How do practitioners (per Young's *Versioning in an Event Sourced System*) handle schema evolution of financial events over a 7–10 year regulatory retention horizon? — *Long-horizon retention is non-negotiable in fintech and is where naive ES adoptions break.*
8. **(Interaction with double-entry accounting)** How does event sourcing compose with double-entry ledger semantics — is it complementary (events as the journal) or duplicative? — *Architectural clarity question for the ledger context specifically.*

### Scope Boundaries

- **In scope**
  - Event sourcing as applied to financial transaction systems (payments, ledger postings, reconciliation, dispute, regulatory reporting).
  - Regulatory regimes: RBI (Master Directions, NPCI reconciliation), GDPR (Articles 5, 17, 25, 32), MiFID II (Article 25, ESMA RTS 22), PCI-DSS v4.0 logging.
  - Storage substrates evaluated: EventStoreDB, Apache Kafka (incl. tiered storage), Axon Server, Postgres-hand-rolled.
  - Patterns adjacent and frequently confused with ES: transactional outbox, CDC-as-events, append-only audit log alongside CRUD.
  - Replay semantics, snapshotting strategies, event schema versioning, GDPR-compatible erasure techniques (crypto-shredding, tokenization).
  - Practitioner failure stories and dissenting voices.
- **Out of scope**
  - Event sourcing for non-financial domains (consumer social, gaming, IoT telemetry) — referenced only when the lesson generalizes.
  - General CQRS as a UI/read-model pattern divorced from event sourcing.
  - Full vendor procurement / TCO modeling — the synthesis informs the decision, it does not replace a sourcing exercise.
  - Specific cloud-provider lock-in analysis beyond the substrate comparison.
  - US-specific regimes (SOX, Reg SCI, FINRA CAT) unless the team confirms US scope is in play — current framing assumes India + EU.

### Timeline & Constraints

- **Decision window**: 2–3 weeks from kickoff (default, since urgency was not explicitly specified). The architectural commitment is high-blast-radius and reversible only at significant cost, so the framing favors **depth over speed**: better to spend the full window than ship a thin synthesis that the compliance team rejects on first read.
- **Implied depth-vs-speed calibration**:
  - Primary-source regulatory citations are non-negotiable (no second-hand summaries of GDPR or RBI rules).
  - Direct quotes from Young / Fowler / Vernon must be sourced from their canonical artifacts, not paraphrased.
  - At least one practitioner failure story per major failure-mode category should be a primary post-mortem, not an aggregator summary.
- **Hard constraints**:
  - Regulatory citations must be current as of 2026 — confirm GDPR + MiFID II + RBI haven't shifted under recent guidance updates.
  - The synthesis must be readable at leadership level (CTO + Compliance Head + VP Engineering) — i.e., the regulatory-fit table and the adopt/partial-adopt/defer paragraph must stand alone without requiring the reader to know what CQRS is.
- **Soft constraints**:
  - Prefer sources with named authors and dates over anonymized vendor marketing.
  - Where Indian-context primary sources are thin (a known gap for RBI/NPCI in English-language technical literature), explicitly flag the gap rather than substitute EU/US sources silently.
